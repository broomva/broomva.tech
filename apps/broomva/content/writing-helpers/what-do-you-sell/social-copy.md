## LinkedIn Post

The "sell shovels and pickaxes" playbook is dead in the AI era.

In the Gold Rush, shovels were physical. Scarce. You couldn't copy one by looking at it. That's why selling tools worked.

But in the AI era, code is infinitely reproducible, deployment is automated, and configuration is a conversation. The shovel copies itself. It digs the mine. It builds the next shovel while you sleep.

When the marginal cost of building software approaches zero, the entire SaaS model — "we run it so you don't have to" — becomes a race to zero.

So what remains scarce?

- Trust — you can't fork a reputation
- Capital — you can't fork money in an account
- Network density — worthless at N=1, valuable at N=10,000
- Accumulated state — history can't be bootstrapped from GitHub
- Accountability — you can't open-source liability

The winners won't sell tools. They'll build the banks, credit bureaus, registries, and insurance companies of the agent economy.

We are living through an unprecedented moment. Never before have humans been this empowered to build whatever they imagine. The barriers weren't lowered — they were removed.

The question is no longer what to sell. The question is what to become.
